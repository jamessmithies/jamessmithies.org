Four talks from 2017

Published on 24 Dec 2017, 10:13 p.m. Filed under digital-humanities
archiving-preservation conference-papers-talks-seminars infrastructure theory

A selection of four talks from 2017, at the Bodleian Library, DH2017 (Montreal), University of Cologne, and DPASSH 2017 (University of Sussex). They provide a good overview of activities in King's Digital Lab this year and - in particular, perhaps - my attempts to work out what it all means.

  • Smithies, J. (2017, November). Systems Development & Applications / Data Lifecycle Management at King’s Digital Lab. Bodleian Library, University of Oxford.
  • Smithies, J., Paul Caton, Ginestra Ferraro, Luis Figueira, Elliott Hall, Neil Jakeman, … Carina Westling. (2017, August). Mechanizing the Humanities? King’s Digital Lab as Critical Experiment. Presented at the DH2017, McGill University, Montreal.
  • Smithies, J. (2017, July). Humans in the Loop: King’s Digital Lab as Socio-technical System. Digital Humanities Institute, University of Cologne.
  • Smithies, J., Sichani, A.-M., & Westling, C. (2017, June). Preserving 30 years of Digital Humanities Work: The Experience of King’s College London Digital Lab. Presented at the DPASSH: Digital Preservation for Social Sciences and Humanities, University of Sussex.
