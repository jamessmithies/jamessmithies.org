<p>A selection of four talks from 2017, at the Bodleian Library, DH2017 (Montreal), University of Cologne, and DPASSH 2017 (University of Sussex). They provide a good overview of activities in King&#39;s Digital Lab this year and - in particular, perhaps - my attempts to work out what it all means.</p>

<ul>
	<li>Smithies, J. (2017, November). <a href="http://jamessmithies.org/blog/2017/12/01/systems-development-application-data-lifecycle-management-kings-digital-lab/"><em>Systems Development &amp; Applications / Data Lifecycle Management at King&rsquo;s Digital Lab</em></a>. Bodleian Library, University of Oxford.</li>
	<li>Smithies, J., Paul Caton, Ginestra Ferraro, Luis Figueira, Elliott Hall, Neil Jakeman, &hellip; Carina Westling. (2017, August). <a href="http://jamessmithies.org/blog/2017/08/17/mechanizing-humanities-kings-digital-lab-critical-experiment/">Mechanizing the Humanities? King&rsquo;s Digital Lab as Critical Experiment</a>. Presented at the DH2017, McGill University, Montreal.</li>
	<li>Smithies, J. (2017, July). <a href="http://jamessmithies.org/blog/2017/08/16/humans-loop-kings-digital-lab-socio-technical-system/"><em>Humans in the Loop: King&rsquo;s Digital Lab as Socio-technical System</em></a>. Digital Humanities Institute, University of Cologne.</li>
	<li>Smithies, J., Sichani, A.-M., &amp; Westling, C. (2017, June). <a href="http://jamessmithies.org/blog/2017/06/23/preserving-30-years-digital-humanities-work-experience-kings-digital-lab/">Preserving 30 years of Digital Humanities Work: The Experience of King&rsquo;s College London Digital Lab</a>. Presented at the DPASSH: Digital Preservation for Social Sciences and Humanities, University of Sussex.</li>
</ul>
