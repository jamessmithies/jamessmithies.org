<p>This is a video of my contribution to the workshop &#39;The Frontiers of DH: Humanities Systems Infrastructure&#39;, presented by the UC Digital Humanities Programme during November 2015. See also <span dir="ltr"><a href="https://youtu.be/jZCvwhaqd7g" target="_blank">Prof. Paul Arthur: Smart Infrastructures for Cultural and Social Research</a>, <a href="https://youtu.be/KHnJCc2Sc4Y" target="_blank">Prof. Alan Liu: Against the Cultural Singularity</a>, and (earlier in the month) <a href="https://youtu.be/FL5pP2ysjU4" target="_blank">Dr. Tim Sherratt: Towards A Manifesto for Tactical DH Research Infrastructure</a>. A series of blog posts about the workshop,&nbsp;by Lucy-Jane Walsh from the UC CEISMIC archive project, starts <a href="http://dh.canterbury.ac.nz/blog/2015/12/13/digital-humanities-infrastructure-workshop/" target="_blank">here</a>.</span></p>

<p><span dir="ltr">The diagram referred to in my seminar is available below. Many thanks to the audience for pointing out inconsistencies and areas for improvement, which are reflected&nbsp;in subsequent drafts.</span></p>

<p>Abstract: Humanists are often resistant to conversations about infrastructure, viewing it as a topic better suited to managers and technical experts. By undertaking a humanities-centered systems analysis of our infrastructure however, we unlock a range of technical, cultural and socio-political issues - and create opportunities to develop a global humanities infrastructure of massive value to future generations.</p>
